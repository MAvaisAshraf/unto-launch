Unto was started as a rural recovery project with the goal to bring life back to neglected pockets of Tuscan land. These fertile grounds sustained rich agriculture for centuries but fell into abandonment following rural depopulation since the ’50s. It is in this setting that our independent farming community was born. Producing a range of superior quality olive oils is the first step on our journey to restore and revive the land and our heritage.

OUR STORY

Unto only looks to high-quality extra virgin olive oil, with low acidity, which keeps intact the characteristic perfumes of cut grass and herbs, with tones of artichoke and fresh almond, giving the oil a unique and well-balanced flavor. The variety of olive trees, also known as “cultivar”, in the area around Florence are mainly Moraiolo, Leccino, Frantoio and Pendolino and Leccio del Corno, but also ancient varieties like Maurino, Rossellino and Correggiolo are present in small numbers. We proudly support producers who try to restore and enhance old cultivars in order to produce a unique product. Thanks to the genuineness of the raw materials and the simple milling process, without filler additives, this extra virgin olive oil has many virtues. It retains its characteristics for many months, including its nutritional components and its great health properties, which have been widely demonstrated by research and scientific studies. The winter pruning of the olive trees starts from the end of February and continues until the end of April. Another pruning takes place in August, adding finishing touches to the pruning and preparing for the autumn harvest to ensure an adequate balance between the production of the plants and to maintain constant productivity over the years. The olive trees receive strictly natural treatments to prevent a number of illnesses and are fertilized in the autumn, following the harvest, with organic fertilizers. Towards the end of October, the olive harvest begins. It is carried out manually or by means of low impact mechanical facilitators with the aim to preserve the conditions of the olives until the moment of pressing, thus limiting natural oxidative processes, fermentation and overheating. The quality of the final product is further ensured by the short holding time until pressing. The olives are taken to the press and processed within a maximum of 12-24 hours after harvest. The pressing takes place at local mills of the highest quality and are rigorously cold-pressed and filtered to remove the presence of minor residual pulp giving the product a greater stability and resistance to ageing.
